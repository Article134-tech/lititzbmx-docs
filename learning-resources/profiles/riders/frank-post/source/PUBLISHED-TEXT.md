# Published source transcription — Frank Post

> Preserved from the supplied Lititz BMX profile text. This transcription records publication wording and does not convert every statement into an independently verified fact.

Rider Profile: Frank Post

Born: 20 April, 1962

Hometown: Watsonville, Ca

Q: What happened during his first race?

A: He won his first moto but fell in the main

Q: What was his first race bike?

A: R&R - given to him by a friend

Q: When did he turn pro?

A: May 1978 at the age of 16

First professional win: May 27, 1979 in Watsonville, CA - Spring National

Retired: Reclassified as an amatuer in 1990

Post retirement careers include: Culinary Chef, Pool Shark, Machinist, and Salesman

“well im a reputed bad guy...but really just mis-understood.”...”I did the best I could, it wasn’t always the best, but it is what I did.” - Frank Post on RoostBMX

Sampling of major wins:

- 1979: UBR Pro Class and Open Expert Grandnational Champion
- 1979: UBR National No. 1 Pro
- 1980: NBA & UBR & ABA Pro Grandnational Champion - the first racer to win the Grand Nationals of three different sanctioning bodies in the same year
