# Published source transcription — Harry Leary

> Preserved from the supplied Lititz BMX profile text. This transcription records publication wording and does not convert every statement into an independently verified fact.

Rider Profile: Harry Leary

22 Feb. 1959 - 7 Sep. 2024

Place of Birth: Lynwood, Ca

Nickname: Turbo

First Race: July 30, 1974

Turned Professional: 1977

Retired as a Pro: 1989

Sampling of Major Wins:

- 1985 - AA Pro Canada Cup 1
- 1986 - Porsche Design Fall Nationals
- 1993 - ABA Vet Pro 1
- 1994 - ABA Vet Pro 1
- 2015 - ROC NAG 1
- 2021 - ROC NAG 1
- 1986 - Winner of The F-1 Challenge
- 1989 - Dual Slalom Mountain Bike World Champion

“More cover shots than any other racer” - Harry Leary (bmxultra.com)

Q: What sort of training makes a good racer?

A: “Consistent! Train a little at a time, and it will add up.”

Q: You have been racing a long time now, what keeps you motivated? (bmxsultra.com)

A: “I am still motivated because I love to compete, and I love to train.”

Q: What was his middle name?

A: Clarence - He was also a Jr.

He “contributed to the BMX industry by collaborating with leading manufacturers such as Cutting Edge, Marzocchi, Balance, and Western State Imports” - Playersbio.com
