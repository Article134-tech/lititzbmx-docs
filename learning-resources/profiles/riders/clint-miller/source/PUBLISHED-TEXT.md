# Published source transcription — Clint Miller

> Preserved from the supplied Lititz BMX profile text. This transcription records publication wording and does not convert every statement into an independently verified fact.

Rider Profile: Clint Miller

Nickname: The Barbarian

Born: 29 April, 1962

Started Racing: 13 years old (1975) at the Covina Valley BMX Track in Covina, Ca. - He did not make the mains.

First Sponsor: Jim Melton Cyclery (JMC) - November 1976

Turned Professional: 1978 at the age of 15

The amount of money he won in his first professional race (1st Place): $17 - at the local Covina track.

Retired in 1986 at age 24.

2005 ABA BMX Hall of Fame Inductee

Sampling of Career Milestones:

- 1976: First National Win - 1976 NBA Grandnationals - East Irvine, Ca
- 1980: Pro Money & Pro Trophy (3rd) JAG World Champion
- 1981: Anglo-American Challenge Invitational Champion - United Kingdom BMX Association (UKBMXA) and IBMXF sanctioned
- 1983: Pro World Champion - IBMXF
- 1984: Qantas BMX Titles Australian Champion

Sponsors:

- 1976-1978: JMC Racing Equipment
- 1978: D.G. Performance Specialties
- 1978-1979: GJS So. Cal
- 1979-1982: Torker BMX Racing Products
- 1983-1984: Kuwahara Cycles, Ltd.
- 1984: Cycle Pro/GHP
- 1985-1986: CW Racing

Amateur Sponsors:

- 1995: DirtWerx

About Clint Miller:

“Not only fast, not only did he have style, but he was everyones friend. And was a mentor to every kid at the track.” - Torker Racing
