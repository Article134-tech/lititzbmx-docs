## Detailed verification

### 1. Greg Hill

The supplied caption states:

> At this time, Greg Hill had just ended his one-man boycott of the ABA and was fresh off of Red Line.

**Answer:** C. Greg Hill.

### 2. True — with a precise scope note

The correct published-quiz disposition is **True**.

The supplied article places 1981 in the emergence of the Pro Cruiser class and describes 24-inch cruisers taking over. A historical account records the first ABA national Pro Cruiser race in January 1981 and says riders accumulated cruiser points during the season.

**Important distinction:** Cruiser racing existed before 1981. The statement is preserved and answered in the narrower context of the 1981 Pro Cruiser points competition described by the source series—not as a claim that no cruiser class existed earlier.

**Supplemental source:** https://nebraskabmxhalloffame.com/tim-lillethorup/

### 3. Steve Johnson

The supplied Team Torker caption states:

> That’s Steve Johnson, Torker’s owner, without a jersey.

**Answer:** B. Steve Johnson.

### 4. JMC pants

The supplied right page begins below the original top edge. The surviving fragment says:

> … what he called a “Young.” JMC always did have the stylish riders, didn’t they?

That fragment belongs to the source discussion associated with the published pants question.

**Answer:** JMC.

**Evidence classification:** Contextually verified. The missing opening words are not reconstructed.

### 5. Bob Morgan

A period-article index describes an ACS Z-Rim test from the September 1980 issue of *BMX Action* as featuring **Trash Can Bob Morgan**.

**Answer:** B. Bob Morgan.

**Supplemental source:** https://www.dreamgate.ne.jp/kuwahara/articles/article.html

### Extra credit — Bell Moto III

> … Premier helmets were laid down to rest when Bell came out with their tough and durable looking Moto III.

**Answer:** Bell Moto III helmets.

The quiz phrase **“the only nogan-knocker to wear”** is preserved as published.
