## Detailed verification

### 1. 31 points

The racing section states:

> Rupe ended up being NBL No. 1 pro for 1984, beating out Greg Hill by 31 points.

**Answer:** 31 points.

### 2. Pistol Pete

The photograph caption states:

> The pride of CW Racing, “Pistol Pete” Loncarevich.

**Answer:** B. Pistol Pete.

### 3. Shaun Texas / Shawn Texas

The article states:

> Shawn Texas, a HUGE up-and-comer who had his ups-and-downs, joined Pro Neck.

The published quiz spells the name **Shaun Texas**.

**Answer:** D. Shaun Texas, preserving the quiz spelling.  
**Source spelling:** Shawn Texas.

### 4. True

The article states:

> He won his second NORA Cup in a row, this being his first one with GHP.

It also notes that people still loved him even without a GT jersey.

**Answer:** True.

### 5. Zeronine

The article states:

> He signed with Zeronine and began to look like a force to be reckoned with.

**Answer:** D. Zeronine.

### Extra credit — Mike Dominguez

The freestyle section states:

> Eddie Fiola was the current King, but some young punk by the name of Mike Dominguez was moving up.

It immediately adds that the two battled it out.

**Answer:** Mike Dominguez.

### Extra extra credit — False

The supplied caption states:

> At the end of the year, he was off Torker and on GT.

The quiz statement instead says he ended the season with his own company, Tommy Brackens Products.

**Answer:** False.

## Critical verification findings

1. All seven published prompts have direct source support.
2. The quiz/article spelling difference **Shaun Texas / Shawn Texas** is preserved and explicitly documented.
3. Question 4 is supported by the article’s wording “his first one with GHP”; the quiz phrases this as his first wearing a GHP jersey.
4. The final true/false statement is contradicted directly by the supplied source: Tommy Brackens ended 1984 on GT.
5. The source identifies Eric Rupe’s NBL No. 1 margin as exactly 31 points.
6. No answer depends on external reconstruction or an unsupported inference.
