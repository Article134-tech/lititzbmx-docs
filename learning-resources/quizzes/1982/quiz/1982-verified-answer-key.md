## Detailed verification

### 1. GT

The source states:

> Nelson Chanady, from Florida, had arrived and delivered. It didn’t take too long before GT picked him up.

**Answer:** B. GT.

### 2. Pontiac Silverdome

The race caption states:

> The race: Pontiac Silverdome.

It then describes the event as:

> the first IBMXF race on American soil

**Answer:** D. Pontiac Silverdome.

### 3. “Pedalin’” Lee Medlin

The photograph caption begins:

> Pedalin’ Lee Medlin.

**Answer:** “Pedalin’.”

### 4. True

The product discussion says:

> GT is said to have sold “close to a million.”

The quiz asks whether GT sold “close to a million” GT Pro bars.

**Answer:** True.

This archive preserves the statement as a claim made by the published source; it does not independently convert the estimate into an audited sales total.

### 5. Darrell Young

The article states:

> Darrell Young became known as one of the smoothest guys to ever grace a BMX track. The guy was silk!

The photograph caption identifies him as:

> Mr. Smooth, himself. Darrell Young

**Answer:** A. Darrell Young.

### Extra credit — shave

The Greg Hill section states:

> Greg OWNED 1982. He also didn’t shave a lot.

**Answer:** Shave.

### Extra extra credit — unresolved arrest question

The published question asks:

> Who was arrested the night before the Pontiac Silverdome races?

The supplied pages 42–43 do not identify the person. Targeted searches using the exact question, the event, the venue, and likely rider names did not locate a sufficiently reliable historical source.

**Answer status:** Unresolved known exception.

**Archival rule:** Do not guess a rider’s name and do not convert oral lore into a factual answer without evidence.

### Extra extra extra credit — Nowhere, Nebraska

The article states:

> And one of the biggest locals-on-the-verge was Greg Grubbs. From outta nowhere (Nowhere, Nebraska), he showed up in a pro main.

**Answer:** Nowhere, Nebraska.
