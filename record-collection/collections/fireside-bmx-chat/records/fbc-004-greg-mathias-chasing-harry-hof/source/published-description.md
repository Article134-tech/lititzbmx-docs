Fireside BMX Chat Podcast: "Chasing Harry" Series - Episode 4 - BMX Team USA’s Greg Mathias Talks About His Friend, Harry Leary, while touring the National BMX Hall of Fame (HoF)

In the fourth episode of the highly anticipated "Chasing Harry" podcast series, BMX Team USA rider Greg Mathias sits down with host Kyle A. Huffman at the iconic National BMX Hall of Fame (HoF) for an in-depth conversation about the legendary BMX racer, Harry Leary. This emotional and inspiring episode explores Harry's incredible legacy and his lasting impact on the sport of BMX.

As Greg and Kyle tour the Hall of Fame, they dive into a collection of unforgettable stories and memorabilia from Harry's career, reflecting on his remarkable achievements and his unforgettable personality.

Greg Mathias opens up about his relationship with Harry Leary, reflecting on how they trained together and how Harry became a mentor. Greg also reveals his thoughts on Harry’s struggles and the deep connection they shared, which ultimately led Greg to become Harry's "safe zone."

Highlights:

1. Harry Leary's Iconic 24” Daylight BMX Bike: The episode kicks off with a discussion of Harry’s famous 24" Daylight BMX bike, which is displayed at the HoF. Harry won the 2022 ROC 1 plate on it.

2. The Story of Harry’s 20” Daylight Bike: Listeners will hear an unforgettable anecdote about Harry’s original 20” Daylight BMX bike—famous for allegedly being run over by a car. Greg and Kyle share their recollections, diving deep into the story about it.

3. The Tragic End of Harry’s Life: The episode takes a somber turn as Greg recounts the heartbreaking events surrounding Harry’s death. Harry’s tragic end in the Arizona desert, from heatstroke after a solo drive, still haunts Greg. In a powerful reflection, Greg shares how BMX lost a racer, true mentor, and ambassador of the sport.

4. The Return of Harry’s Stolen BMX Bike: Listeners will be riveted by the story of how Greg helped recover Harry's stolen loaner 20” Daylight BMX bike after a six-month effort. It’s a tale of persistence and the deep bond shared by BMX riders, as Greg worked alongside Rob Porter to return the bike to its rightful place at Daylight BMX.

5. Harry Leary’s Enduring Influence on BMX: Greg Mathias emphasizes Harry’s lasting influence on BMX, both as a rider and a businessman. From Harry’s years of training to his impactful role as the face of Diamondback and Western Sports, Greg paints a picture of a man whose legacy continues to shape the sport.

📚 About Lititz BMX

Lititz BMX is an independent BMX preservation project dedicated to documenting and preserving the people, bikes, artifacts, race history, and stories that shaped the sport.

What began as a private collection has evolved into an expanding digital archive featuring hundreds of artifacts, rider profiles, interviews, educational resources, and historical research spanning BMX’s earliest days through today.

Every artifact tells a story. Every rider leaves a legacy. Every piece of BMX history deserves to be preserved. Each recording helps preserve BMX history by documenting the people, artifacts, and stories that might otherwise be lost to time.
