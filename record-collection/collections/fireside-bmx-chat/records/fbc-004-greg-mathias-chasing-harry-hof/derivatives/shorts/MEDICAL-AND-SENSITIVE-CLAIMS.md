# Medical and Sensitive-Claim Qualifications

This derivative set preserves oral-history and publication claims; it does not independently diagnose, adjudicate, or certify them.

## CTE and head-injury statements

GM-009, GM-027, and GM-028 discuss concussions, childhood head injuries, and suspected Chronic Traumatic Encephalopathy. The interview states that friends/family believed Harry Leary may have had CTE and also states that the family did not have him tested. **No independent medical record or CTE diagnosis is supplied.** All such statements remain attributed to the speakers and source descriptions.

## Death and timeline statements

GM-007, GM-011, and GM-012A include recollections about last contact, search timing, location, heat exposure, and estimated timing of death. The package preserves those statements as interview/publication claims. It does not replace an official death record, medical examiner finding, or independently verified timeline.

## Theft and recovery statements

GM-012B and GM-013 preserve recollections about a bicycle being removed from a vehicle, jurisdiction issues, attempted recovery, and eventual return. These are attributed interview/publication claims unless separately documented elsewhere.

## Archival rule

Sensitive claims about health, death, behavior, ownership, theft, motive, or business history must not be silently converted from recollection into institutional fact. The recording remains authoritative for what was said; separate evidence would be required for independent verification.
