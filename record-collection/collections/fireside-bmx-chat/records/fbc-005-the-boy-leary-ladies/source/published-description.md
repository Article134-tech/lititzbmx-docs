Lititz BMX Presents Episode Five of “Fireside BMX Chat”: A Heartfelt Tribute to BMX Legend Harry Leary from the Women Who Knew Him Best

In a profoundly personal and emotionally rich fifth episode of Fireside BMX Chat, host Kyle A. Huffman invites listeners into an intimate conversation with the women closest to BMX icon Harry Leary—his mother, Beverly, and his sisters, Linda and Cammie. This episode, presented by Lititz BMX, transcends the world of racing and trophies, offering a deeply human portrait of one of BMX’s most legendary riders.

Centered around a poster board inspired by Harry’s Mother’s memories and crafted by the Leary family to honor Harry’s memory, this episode explores the stories, laughter, and emotions behind the man known to the BMX world—but remembered by his family simply as Harry.

## A Mother’s Love, A Poet’s Heart

In the episode's emotional centerpiece, Beverly Leary reads a touching poem titled “The Boy,” inspired by her own reflections and penned in honor of her son. Her words paint a vivid portrait of a mother’s love and the quiet ache of watching a son grow into a public figure.

“I’d be lying if I said I didn’t miss the boy he was before BMX fully claimed him... I trust that he is at peace, riding in God’s hands, with the same love and passion he carried throughout his life.”

Harry’s youngest sister, Cammie, shares a moving eulogy that reflects on their childhood, offering “a window into their family,” as Kyle describes it. Her words recall moments of play, mischief, and the special bond only siblings understand—especially those who shared a home filled with both chaos and deep love.

Linda, recites a heartfelt poem she wrote many years ago, a piece that may feel dated in tone but remains undeniably authentic. It stands as a snapshot of a different time and a reminder of the sincerity with which she “celebrated” Harry.

In a touching and spontaneous moment, Kyle invites the Leary Ladies to play a game he calls “One Word.” Each woman is asked to describe Harry using just one word—not the BMX icon, but the son and brother they knew personally. The result is raw, powerful, and deeply human.

Harry Leary was human. He loved, he laughed, and—yes—he cried. Listeners will hear the family speak candidly about his emotional side, breaking down any remaining wall between the icon and the man.

“He cried. He cried a lot,” Beverly says, “just like any other human being.”

Hosted with warmth and curiosity by Kyle A. Huffman, this recording iss an archival journey into the soul of BMX. Supported by Lititz BMX, an exploratory concept in BMX historical preservation, the show aims to keep alive the personal legacies of those who shaped the sport far beyond their race results.

📚 About Lititz BMX

Lititz BMX is an independent BMX preservation project dedicated to documenting and preserving the people, bikes, artifacts, race history, and stories that shaped the sport.

What began as a private collection has grown into an expanding digital archive featuring hundreds of artifacts, rider profiles, interviews, educational resources, and historical research spanning BMX’s earliest days through today.

Every artifact tells a story. Every rider leaves a legacy. Every piece of BMX history deserves to be preserved. Each recording helps preserve BMX history by documenting the people, artifacts, and stories that might otherwise be lost to time.
