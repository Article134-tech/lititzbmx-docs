# Supplied Public Description

> Preserved as supplied by Kyle A. Huffman on July 20, 2026. Truncated Google Sites display URLs are retained as part of the captured publication record.

In this special audio podcast episode, Kyle steps completely out of his comfort zone and into an archery range for a first-ever lesson with Jessica Reveal—an accomplished archer, hunter, and the niece of BMX legend Harry Leary.

What begins as a simple archery lesson quickly turns into an honest, thoughtful, and sometimes funny conversation about family, mentorship, safety, learning through failure, and what it was really like growing up with one of the most influential (and infamous) figures in BMX history.

## 🏹 Episode Concept

Kyle mentioned wanting to learn archery during a conversation with Linda, who connected him with her daughter Jessica. The two meet at an archery range where Jessica teaches Kyle the fundamentals—stance, safety, nocking, drawing, aiming, and release—while sharing stories about her uncle Harry Leary along the way.

## 🔥 Topics & Questions Discussed

First-time archery basics and range safety

Archery equipment breakdown for beginners

Learning new skills with past injuries

Teaching styles and mentorship

Growing up with BMX legend Harry Leary

Favorite family stories and “we’re in trouble” moments

Lessons learned from Harry—both what to do and what not to do

Harry Leary as an uncle vs. Harry Leary the BMX icon

The elbow incident and its impact on family

Commitment to BMX and its personal cost

Finding closure, legacy, and moving forward

One-word reflections and shared secrets

The mental side of archery and focus

📚 About Lititz BMX

Lititz BMX is an independent BMX preservation project dedicated to documenting and preserving the people, bikes, artifacts, race history, and stories that shaped the sport.

What began as a private collection has grown into an expanding digital archive featuring hundreds of artifacts, rider profiles, interviews, educational resources, and historical research spanning BMX’s earliest days through today.

Every artifact tells a story. Every rider leaves a legacy. Every piece of BMX history deserves to be preserved. Each recording helps preserve BMX history by documenting the people, artifacts, and stories that might otherwise be lost to time.
