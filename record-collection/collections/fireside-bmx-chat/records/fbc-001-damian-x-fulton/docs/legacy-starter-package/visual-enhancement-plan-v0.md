# Visually Enhanced Edition — Editorial Plan

## Core rule

The enhanced edition supplements the complete interview. It must never imply that supporting visuals were present in the original scene or that later evidence changes what the speakers actually said.

## Suggested visual lanes

### Interview setting
- supplied episode thumbnail
- beach / bonfire audio-theater title treatment
- unobtrusive speaker identifiers
- chapter cards

### Harry Leary discussion
Potential material, subject to source and rights review:
- verified Harry Leary photographs
- Diamondback advertisements featuring Leary
- Radical Rick artwork in which Leary appears
- SCG shoe artwork discussed in the episode
- BMX Hall of Fame vintage-race poster discussed by Damian

### Damian Fulton career discussion
- BMX Plus! material
- Radical Rick publication imagery
- Hot Wheels, Masters of the Universe, Mattel, and Marvel references
- advertising-era photographs, portfolios, storyboards, or approved examples

### BMX history context
- Joar / Flight pad imagery
- representative Diamondback, Kuwahara, SE, Huffy, GT, Haro, and Boss BMX material
- magazine covers and test-bike pages
- timeline markers for BMX, mountain bikes, X Games, Olympics, and big-wheel bike-life culture

## Documentation required for every visual
- asset filename
- creator / copyright holder
- source
- date or approximate date
- rights or permission status
- statement illustrated
- contemporaneous or retrospective
- uncertainty or identification issue

## On-screen treatment
- keep interview audio continuous unless editorially necessary
- introduce visuals at the exact subject transition
- use lower thirds sparingly
- avoid decorative images with no documentary value
- label reenactments, later photographs, and illustrative material
- include source credits in the video and repository
