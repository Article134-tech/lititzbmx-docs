🔥 *Fireside BMX Chat: Greg Hill Talks Garage Floors, BMX Legends & Epoxy Life* 🔥

Welcome to the premiere episode of **Fireside BMX Chat**, hosted by none other than **Kyle A. Huffman of Lititz BMX**—and yes, his adorable 18-month-old daughter is along for the ride! 🍼💥

In this episode, BMX Hall of Famer and industry icon *Greg Hill* drops by Kyle’s house—not to hit a track, but to bid on his garage floor*! Greg, now the proud Owner/Operator of **Epoxy Surface Professionals* (serving the Los Angeles region), shares some seriously smooth insights—both about *epoxy flooring* and his **legendary BMX past**.

We cover it all:
✅ Greg’s flooring process, pricing, warranties & what real epoxy actually is
📸 Why Greg takes so many photos of garages he works on
📞 How to hire Greg (yes, we’ve got the contact info!)
🧰 Does Greg work solo or have a crew?

And then... things take a nostalgic turn. 👀

🏁 Greg opens up about old-school BMX memories:

His friendship (and rivalry?) with *Harry Leary*
Did he owe Harry a trip into the wall?
Food fights on the freeway 🍔🚗💥
The *Little Rascals*-style club he may or may not have run with his wife
What he really thinks about being the inspiration behind *“The Leary” jump* 😲
Practicing gates with *Stu Thomsen**, **Clint Miller**, and **Harry* in his own driveway
1985 Grands, *Pete Loncarevich**, and what came *after the end of GHP
And most importantly… *Is Greg Hill happy today?*

This episode is packed with BMX gold and garage goals. Whether you're into **race stories**, **garage upgrades**, or just want to hear two generations of Huffmans (and a legend) hang out—this one’s for you.

📚 About Lititz BMX

Lititz BMX is an independent BMX preservation project dedicated to documenting and preserving the people, bikes, artifacts, race history, and stories that shaped the sport.

What began as a private collection has grown into an expanding digital archive featuring hundreds of artifacts, rider profiles, interviews, educational resources, and historical research spanning BMX’s earliest days through today.

Every artifact tells a story. Every rider leaves a legacy. Every piece of BMX history deserves to be preserved. Each recording helps preserve BMX history by documenting the people, artifacts, and stories that might otherwise be lost to time.
