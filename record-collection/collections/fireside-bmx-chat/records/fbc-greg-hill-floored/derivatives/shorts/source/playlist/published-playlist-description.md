# Published Playlist Description

> Preserved verbatim from the user-supplied live playlist record on 2026-07-22.

This playlist brings together every short clip from Lititz BMX's conversation with BMX legend Greg Hill, preserving individual stories that might otherwise remain buried inside a full-length interview.

Each short focuses on a specific memory, lesson, or moment from Greg's BMX career—from growing up alongside Harry Leary, Stu Thomsen, and Clint Miller, to GHP, the BMX industry, racing mindset, business lessons, and the friendships that helped shape one of BMX's most influential eras.

Rather than watching one long interview, these clips allow viewers to explore individual stories while preserving them as searchable historical recordings for future generations.

Topics throughout this series include:

• Greg Hill's friendship with Harry Leary
• Practicing gates with Stu Thomsen and Clint Miller
• GHP BMX
• The history behind "The Leary" jump
• BMX business and frame manufacturing
• Racing mindset and winning championships
• Diamond Back team stories
• BMX industry history
• Life after professional BMX
• Garage flooring, entrepreneurship, and business philosophy

Each recording has been preserved as part of the Lititz BMX Archive, an independent BMX preservation project dedicated to documenting the people, artifacts, stories, and history that shaped the sport.

📚 About Lititz BMX

Lititz BMX is an independent BMX preservation project dedicated to documenting and preserving the people, bikes, artifacts, race history, and stories that shaped the sport.

What began as a private collection has grown into an expanding digital archive featuring hundreds of artifacts, rider profiles, interviews, educational resources, and historical research spanning BMX’s earliest days through today.

Every artifact tells a story. Every rider leaves a legacy. Every piece of BMX history deserves to be preserved. Each recording helps preserve BMX history by documenting the people, artifacts, and stories that might otherwise be lost to time.
