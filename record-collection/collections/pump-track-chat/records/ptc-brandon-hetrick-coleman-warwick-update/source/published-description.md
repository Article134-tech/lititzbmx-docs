Pump Track Chat returns with Brandon Hetrick for an inside look at the Coleman Bike Park build in Lebanon, Pennsylvania—and an update on the effort to bring a community pump track to Lititz and Warwick Township.

Brandon shares what it took to move the Coleman Memorial Park project forward, why community relationships matter, and what Lititz can learn from the people, planning, construction, and cooperation behind the finished park.

In this episode, Kyle A. Huffman and Brandon discuss:

• How the Coleman Bike Park project began
• Working with park boards, municipalities, builders, and community supporters
• Why finding the right person—and continuing to show up—can change a project
• Asphalt, concrete, and dirt pump-track construction
• Designing for bicycles, scooters, skateboards, families, and adaptive riders
• The memorial story connected to Coleman Bike Park
• The current Warwick Township pump-track process
• Possible locations and the township’s continuing feasibility review
• The potential for a future partnership involving Rock Lititz
• Why pump tracks can become lasting community and regional assets

The conversation also explores the difference between municipal and privately supported projects, lessons learned from successful and unsuccessful pump-track designs, and Brandon’s belief that Lititz and Warwick Township are moving in the right direction.

“Just keep showing up, and eventually you’ll change the world.”

📚 About Lititz BMX

Lititz BMX is an independent BMX preservation project dedicated to documenting and preserving the people, bikes, artifacts, race history, and stories that shaped the sport.

What began as a private collection has grown into an expanding digital archive featuring hundreds of artifacts, rider profiles, interviews, educational resources, and historical research spanning BMX’s earliest days through today.

Every artifact tells a story. Every rider leaves a legacy. Every piece of BMX history deserves to be preserved. Each recording helps preserve BMX history by documenting the people, artifacts, and stories that might otherwise be lost to time.
