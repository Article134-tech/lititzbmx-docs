## About this book

The project began with resistance to a widely available “Walmart bike” and a question: **what could it become if the label were ignored?** Over ten chapters, the 2026 Huffy HX Pro Cruiser moves from unopened box to working archive object—shaped by practical learning, Harry Leary provenance, #OperationDIRTWERX continuity, Lititz BMX identity, pump-track advocacy, anxiety, public submission, and use.

This GitHub edition treats the campaign as a digital book. The original chapter sequence and wording remain the center of the experience. Social-media captures and full Google Sites page records are preserved as illustrations and evidence, while provenance, component history, chronology, claim qualifications, structured data, and file fixity provide the durable archival layer underneath.

## The complete arc

1. Resistance becomes possibility.
2. The stock bicycle is evaluated before modification.
3. Harry Leary’s seat and seat post change the project’s meaning.
4. Every component must justify its place.
5. The bike is consciously connected to the Lititz BMX Archive.
6. Performance, preservation, and intention begin setting limits on one another.
7. #OperationDIRTWERX hardware continues physically into a new documented life.
8. The bicycle leaves the stand and proves itself through motion.
9. The build expands into community advocacy, learning, and continued evolution.
10. The bicycle reaches a mature configuration and the builder overcomes the final barrier of public submission.

The Afterword records the accepted Sugar Cayne publication. The Postscript follows the bike into active field use at Coleman Bike Park while Lititz BMX documents another chapter of BMX history.

## Critical evidence boundaries

- The Huffy was not owned or ridden by Harry Leary.
- Harry Leary’s seat and seat post were incorporated through Linda Leary Taylor.
- The Harry-linked chain was considered but not installed.
- DIRTWERX-related grip hardware represents physical continuity between documented projects.
- Sugar Cayne ratings and engagement figures are capture-time snapshots, not permanent totals or contest wins.
- The ride assessments are first-person use records rather than controlled product testing.
- Original campaign language, source screenshots, and later archival explanation remain separate.

## Preservation statement

The bike is preserved here not because it began as rare, but because the documented process gave it meaning. It became a working extension of Lititz BMX: ridden, changed, questioned, connected to earlier history, shared publicly, and carried forward into new work.
