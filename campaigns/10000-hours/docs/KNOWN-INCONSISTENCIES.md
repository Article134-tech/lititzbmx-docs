# Known Inconsistencies and Source Gaps

## Friends with the Monster

The source card visible on the left side of the supplied page capture supports the entry titled **Friends with the Monster** and begins:

> “I am friends with the monster that sits at my gate. Get along with the taunting that spews from hi…”

The visible text is truncated by the original social-media presentation.

The published text on the right side of the Google Sites page instead repeats the wording from **The Greats Rode a Lot**:

> “The greats weren’t great because at birth they could ride, the greats were great because they rode a lot.”

The visible embedded video is also a Macklemore **Ten Thousand Hours** performance. The archive preserves all three elements and does not silently replace one with an assumed correction.

## Ride Through Fire capture gap

A standalone social-post image and the full page URL were supplied for **Ride Through Fire**, but a dedicated full-page Google Sites screenshot was not supplied. The record is complete enough to establish its existence, wording, imagery and inspiration link, but its page-capture field remains intentionally empty.

## None to Blame duplicate submission

The **None to Blame** page was supplied twice during source gathering. The images are separate screenshots of the same page, not separate campaign entries. One is preserved as the canonical page capture. The duplicate does not increase the campaign count.

## Inspiration attribution differences

Some pages explicitly state an artist and song. Other pages provide only an embedded video or external link. The register distinguishes:

- `stated_on_page`
- `visible_in_embed_not_stated_in_page_text`
- `not_stated_on_page`
- `page_text_mismatch`

Visible embed titles are recorded as presentation evidence, not treated as stronger attribution than the source page itself.

## Title and capitalization variation

The supplied material uses **10,000 hours**, **10,000 Hours**, **10,000 Hour Series**, and **The 10,000 Hour Series**. The archive preserves those forms inside the records while using **10,000 Hours of BMX Inspiration Mixed Tape** as the normalized GitHub exhibition title.
